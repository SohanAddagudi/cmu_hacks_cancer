Types of Cancer

Adrenocortical Carcinoma

Adrenocortical carcinoma is a rare type of cancer that affects the outer layer of the adrenal glands, responsible for hormone production.

Bladder Urothelial Carcinoma

Bladder urothelial carcinoma is the most common type of bladder cancer, originating in the cells lining the bladder.

Breast Invasive Carcinoma

Breast invasive carcinoma refers to cancer that has spread from the milk ducts or lobules into nearby breast tissue.

Cervical Squamous Cell Carcinoma

This type of cervical cancer develops in the squamous cells lining the cervix, typically due to HPV infection.

Cholangiocarcinoma

Cholangiocarcinoma is a cancer that arises from the bile ducts, which carry bile from the liver to the small intestine.

Colon Adenocarcinoma

Colon adenocarcinoma is the most common form of colorectal cancer, beginning in the mucus-producing glands of the colon.

Diffuse Large B-cell Lymphoma

Diffuse large B-cell lymphoma is a fast-growing type of non-Hodgkin lymphoma that affects B lymphocytes, a type of white blood cell.

Esophageal Carcinoma

Esophageal carcinoma is a cancer that occurs in the tissues of the esophagus, which carries food and liquid to the stomach.

Glioblastoma Multiforme

Glioblastoma multiforme is an aggressive type of brain cancer that forms in the glial cells supporting the brain's nerve cells.

Head and Neck Squamous Cell Carcinoma

This cancer affects the squamous cells in the mucosal surfaces of the head and neck, including the mouth, throat, and nose.

Kidney Chromophobe

Kidney chromophobe carcinoma is a rare form of kidney cancer that typically has a better prognosis than other kidney cancers.

Kidney Renal Cell Carcinoma

Kidney renal cell carcinoma is the most common type of kidney cancer, developing in the small tubes that filter blood.

Kidney Renal Papillary Cell Carcinoma

This type of kidney cancer forms in the lining of the kidney's tubules, often leading to slow-growing tumors.

Acute Myeloid Leukemia

Acute myeloid leukemia is a fast-growing cancer of the blood and bone marrow, affecting immature white blood cells.

Brain Lower Grade Glioma

Brain lower grade gliomas are slower-growing brain tumors that arise from the glial cells supporting the nervous system.

Liver Hepatocellular Carcinoma

Liver hepatocellular carcinoma is the most common type of primary liver cancer, often linked to chronic liver disease and cirrhosis.

Lung Adenocarcinoma

Lung adenocarcinoma is the most common form of non-small cell lung cancer, often found in the outer areas of the lung.

Lung Squamous Cell Carcinoma

This type of lung cancer originates in the flat squamous cells lining the airways of the lung.

Mesothelioma

Mesothelioma is a rare cancer that forms in the lining of the lungs, abdomen, or heart, typically caused by asbestos exposure.

Ovarian Serous Cystadenocarcinoma

Ovarian serous cystadenocarcinoma is the most common type of ovarian cancer, forming in the tissue covering the ovary.

Pancreatic Adenocarcinoma

Pancreatic adenocarcinoma arises in the pancreas's ducts, where digestive enzymes are produced.

Pheochromocytoma and Paraganglioma

These are rare tumors that develop in the adrenal glands or nearby nerve tissues, affecting hormone production.

Prostate Adenocarcinoma

Prostate adenocarcinoma is the most common type of prostate cancer, originating in the gland cells of the prostate.

Rectum Adenocarcinoma

Rectum adenocarcinoma is a form of colorectal cancer that develops in the tissue lining the rectum.

Sarcoma

Sarcomas are a group of cancers that originate in the bones, muscles, fat, or connective tissues.

Skin Cutaneous Melanoma

Melanoma is a skin cancer that develops in the melanocytes, the cells that produce pigment in the skin.

Stomach Adenocarcinoma

Stomach adenocarcinoma is the most common type of stomach cancer, developing in the glandular cells of the stomach lining.

Testicular Germ Cell Tumors

Testicular germ cell tumors are the most common form of testicular cancer, often developing in young men.

Thyroid Carcinoma

Thyroid carcinoma is the most common cancer of the thyroid gland, affecting hormone regulation in the body.

Thymoma

Thymoma is a rare cancer of the thymus gland, located in the upper chest, and is often associated with autoimmune diseases.

Uterine Corpus Endometrial Carcinoma

This type of uterine cancer forms in the lining of the uterus and is commonly referred to as endometrial cancer.

Uterine Carcinosarcoma

Uterine carcinosarcoma is a rare and aggressive cancer that has features of both carcinoma and sarcoma.

Uveal Melanoma

Uveal melanoma is a rare cancer that arises in the eye's uveal tract, affecting structures such as the iris and choroid.

Normal

No cancer detected. This category indicates a healthy state.
